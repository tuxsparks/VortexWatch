TIV 2 Purchased for $5,000: The Resurrection of a Legend

In a shocking turn of events for the storm chasing community, the iconic Tornado Intercept Vehicle 2 (TIV 2) was sold at a private auction yesterday for a mere $5,000. The vehicle, once the star of IMAX documentaries, had been sitting in disrepair for several years.

The buyer, a private weather enthusiast and mechanical engineer, has pledged to fully restore the 14,000-pound beast. Plans include upgrading the hydraulic skirting system and replacing the aging armor plating with modern carbon-steel composites.

Meteorologists are still analyzing data from last night's historic event. Preliminary radar surveys indicate the tornado reached a maximum width of 2.7 miles, making it one of the largest ever recorded by mobile Doppler units.

Next-Gen Satellite "Aegis-7" Successfully Deployed

NASA and NOAA have confirmed that the Aegis-7 weather satellite has reached its geostationary orbit. This new hardware provides 30-second refresh rates on lightning detection and cloud-top cooling trends.

This deployment marks a new era in hyper-local forecasting, allowing meteorologists to predict "tornadogenesis" with much higher accuracy than the previous GOES series satellites.
